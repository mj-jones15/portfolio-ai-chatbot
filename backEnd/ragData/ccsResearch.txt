College, Research
As an AI Researcher with the University of Kentucky Center for Computational Sciences, I’ve contributed to multiple projects supporting ACCESS (Advanced Cyberinfrastructure Coordination Ecosystem: Services & Support), a nationwide National Science Foundation initiative that provides researchers with high-performance computing and data resources.

Our team focuses on improving how researchers interact with these systems through applied AI. I helped develop an initial question-answering chatbot trained on hundreds of technical documents, then expanded that work into a more advanced system capable of assisting with complex, proposal-related tasks. This includes building a retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) model that helps users draft allocation abstracts and proposals based on prior successful submissions.

Beyond chatbot development, I’ve worked on several supporting AI systems: designing an MCP-based classifier that routes user queries to specialized agents, developing an LLM-driven tagging pipeline to categorize proposals by abstract content, and improving model performance through document verification and iterative retraining.

Through these projects, I’ve gained hands-on experience building end-to-end AI systems—working with tools like Ollama, ChromaDB, and Hugging Face—and developed a strong understanding of embeddings, metadata design, and scalable vector search. More broadly, this work has reinforced how critical data quality, structure, and retrieval strategy are to building reliable, high-impact AI applications.
