A big part of why I chose to attend the University of Kentucky was its athletic culture—especially basketball. I grew up a lifelong Kentucky fan thanks to my family’s deep roots in the state, and now that I’m a student, I attend as many UK basketball games as possible. My love for Big Blue Nation extends beyond basketball as well, and I regularly support Kentucky athletics across multiple sports, especially baseball.

Outside of academics, I am heavily involved in the University of Kentucky’s premier men’s a cappella group, the acoUstiKats. Over the past several years, I have served as the group’s Social Media Manager, helping grow our online presence and promote performances across campus and the Lexington community. I also perform with the group at a wide range of events, from singing the national anthem at Keeneland to appearances at Kentucky basketball games and major campus concerts. This year, we were proud to compete in the 2026 ICCA Midwest Quarterfinals—the collegiate a cappella competition featured in Pitch Perfect.

I am also a member and Treasurer of Tau Beta Pi, the Engineering Honor Society. Through this role, I have strengthened my leadership and organizational skills while collaborating with highly motivated engineering students across disciplines.
